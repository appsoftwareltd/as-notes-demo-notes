# Watering

Watering your [[Plants]] is, in theory, the simplest thing in the world. In practice, it is the source of approximately 90% of plant-related crimes against nature. Both [[Overwatering]] and underwatering are very real problems, and plants will not tell you which one is happening until you've already made it much worse.

## The Golden Rule

> Stick your finger into the [[Soil]] up to the first knuckle. If it's damp, don't water. If it's dry, water.

That's it. That's the whole page. The rest is elaboration for people who enjoy reading.

## How Much Water?

Water thoroughly — enough that water drains freely from the bottom of the pot. Then stop. Do not give it a "little extra." It doesn't need a little extra.

After watering, allow the [[Soil]] to drain fully. [[Plants]] sitting in standing water are on a fast track to [[Root Rot]]. See [[Overwatering]] for the full horror story.

## How Often?

Depends entirely on:
- The type of [[Plant]] (a [[Succulent]] and a [[Monstera]] have wildly different opinions)
- The size of the pot
- The season (see [[Season Guide]] — [[Plants]] drink much less in winter)
- How warm and dry your home is
- Whether you have south-facing windows

As a rough guide:
- **Summer**: every 1–2 weeks for most houseplants
- **Winter**: every 3–4 weeks, sometimes less

## Water Quality

Most tap water is fine. If your plant is being dramatic about it (often the case with [[Succulent]] varieties), you can leave tap water in a jug overnight to let chlorine evaporate, or use rainwater if you have it.

## Tools

A [[Watering Can]] with a long, narrow spout gives you better control and less mess. You're not a firefighter. No need to just blast water everywhere.
