# Root Rot

[[Root Rot]] is what happens when roots stay wet for too long and pathogenic fungi move in. It is, without overstating it, the worst thing that can regularly happen to your [[Plants]]. While [[Yellow Leaves]] and [[Pests]] are annoyances, [[Root Rot]] is genuinely threatening and will kill a [[Plant]] if left untreated.

It almost always comes from [[Overwatering]].

## How It Happens

Wet [[Soil]] → depleted oxygen → roots begin to die → fungi (*Pythium*, *Phytophthora*, *Fusarium*, etc.) colonise the dead roots → fungal spread to healthy roots → by the time you notice something's wrong, a significant portion of the root system may already be gone.

The above process can happen in as little as a few weeks in the wrong conditions.

## Signs of Root Rot

- Persistent wilting despite wet [[Soil]]
- [[Yellow Leaves]] or sudden leaf drop
- Musty or foul smell from the pot
- When you remove the [[Plant]]: roots that are brown or black, mushy, and come apart easily (healthy roots are firm and usually white or tan)

## Treatment

[[Root Rot]] can be saved if caught early enough. This requires action, not optimism.

1. **Remove the [[Plant]] from its pot**. Gently.
2. **Wash the roots** under lukewarm water to remove old [[Soil]] and expose the damage.
3. **Trim all affected roots** with clean, sharp scissors — cut back to where the root is firm and healthy. Sterilise your scissors between cuts.
4. **Dust the cuts** with powdered cinnamon (a mild antifungal) or a commercial fungicide.
5. **Repot into fresh, dry [[Soil]]** — see [[Soil]] for suitable draining mixes. Do NOT reuse the old [[Soil]].
6. **Wait before [[Watering]]** — leave it for several days to allow cut roots to callous before adding moisture.
7. **Move to bright indirect [[Sunlight]]** and good airflow.

If more than two-thirds of the root system is gone: the [[Plant]] probably won't survive, but trying the above gives it a chance.

## Prevention

- Use [[Soil]] with good drainage
- Ensure pots have drainage holes
- Never let [[Plants]] sit in standing water
- Check the [[Soil]] before [[Watering]] every single time
- See [[Overwatering]] for full prevention guidance
