# Snake Plant

*Dracaena trifasciata* (formerly *Sansevieria*) — the [[Snake Plant]]. Also known as Mother-in-Law's Tongue, a nickname that requires no explanation once you've seen the upright, sharply pointed leaves. The [[Snake Plant]] is one of the most popular houseplants in the world, and it has earned that status by being almost entirely impossible to kill.

It will outlast your interest in it. It will outlast your forgetfulness. It will, in all probability, outlast you.

## What Makes It Special

The [[Snake Plant]] is one of the few [[Plants]] that continues to convert CO₂ into oxygen at night, making it a popular choice for bedrooms. Whether this meaningfully improves sleep quality is debated. What is not debated is that having a plant that looks this architectural and dramatic requires almost no effort on your part, which is an excellent deal.

The leaves are stiff, upright, and patterned with horizontal bands of dark and light green — sometimes with yellow margins. They look like they were designed, which is probably why interior designers love them.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Almost any — low to bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; avoid harsh direct sun |
| [[Watering]] | Every 2–6 weeks; allow [[Soil]] to dry completely between waterings |
| [[Soil]] | Fast-draining [[Soil]]; cactus/succulent mix works perfectly |
| Humidity | Indifferent. Dry air is fine. Everything is fine |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] 2–3 times during spring/summer; skip winter |

## Watering: The Make-or-Break Factor

The [[Snake Plant]] stores water in its leaves and rhizomes, which means it is extraordinarily drought-tolerant. The one thing guaranteed to kill it is [[Overwatering]]. Sitting in wet [[Soil]] leads to [[Root Rot]] with alarming speed.

In winter especially, watering every 6–8 weeks — or even less — is entirely appropriate. If the leaves start to look slightly wrinkled, water it. Otherwise, leave it alone.

## Light

The [[Snake Plant]] tolerates low light better than almost any other houseplant. It will survive in a windowless corridor, a dim bathroom, or a north-facing room where other [[Plants]] wouldn't dare. It grows much faster in bright indirect [[Sunlight]], but it will not complain about either arrangement.

## Common Problems

- **Mushy base or leaves**: [[Root Rot]] from [[Overwatering]] — act fast, remove affected roots, repot in fresh dry [[Soil]]
- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: Typically [[Overwatering]], or occasionally cold temperatures — stop watering and move somewhere warmer
- **Brown tips**: Low humidity, inconsistent [[Watering]], or fluoride in tap water — trim the tips, switch to filtered water
- **Slow or no growth**: Completely normal in low light or winter. It's resting. Do not intervene

## Varieties

The classic is *Dracaena trifasciata* — dark green with light banding and a yellow edge. Other notable varieties include:

- **'Laurentii'** — the yellow-edged one you've definitely seen in every waiting room
- **'Moonshine'** — silvery-grey leaves, striking and slightly eerie
- **'Cylindrica'** — cylindrical leaves instead of flat, looks almost like a sculptural art piece
- **'Hahnii' (Bird's Nest)** — compact rosette form, perfect for small spaces

## Propagation

Divide at the rhizome when [[Repotting]], or take leaf cuttings placed in [[Soil]] or water. Note: variegated varieties like 'Laurentii' will lose their yellow edges if propagated from leaf sections — you must divide at the rhizome to preserve the variegation.
