# Boston Fern

*Nephrolepis exaltata* — the Boston Fern. Lush, billowing, deeply Victorian in its aesthetic, and deeply unforgiving if you live somewhere with dry central heating. The [[Boston Fern]] is not a plant for the forgetful or the low-humidity household. It is, however, spectacular when happy, and it will fill a hanging basket like nothing else.

## What It Actually Wants

Unlike most popular houseplants that ask you to let them dry out between waterings, the [[Boston Fern]] wants to stay consistently moist. Not waterlogged — moist. It also wants humidity. High humidity. The kind of humidity your bathroom has, or a room with a humidifier running nearby.

If you do not provide humidity, you will be rewarded with brown, crispy fronds that drop their leaflets all over your floor. This is the [[Boston Fern]]'s primary form of protest.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; tolerates lower light but will grow more slowly |
| [[Watering]] | Keep [[Soil]] consistently moist; water when the top inch feels barely dry |
| [[Soil]] | Peat-based or coco coir potting mix with good drainage |
| Humidity | High — ideally 50–80%. Mist daily or use a humidifier/pebble tray |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] every 2–4 weeks in spring and summer |

## The Humidity Problem

This is it. This is the whole challenge. Most UK homes in winter run at 30–40% humidity, which is not enough for a [[Boston Fern]]. Solutions:

- **Humidity tray**: Fill a tray with pebbles and water; sit the pot on top (above the waterline)
- **Grouping plants**: A cluster of [[Plants]] raises local humidity slightly
- **Humidifier**: The nuclear option and the most effective one
- **Bathroom**: If you have a bright bathroom, put it there. It will thrive.

## Common Problems

- **Brown, crispy fronds**: Low humidity or inconsistent [[Watering]]. Increase misting and check the moisture level daily.
- **Yellowing fronds**: [[Overwatering]], or occasionally too much direct [[Sunlight]]
- **Shedding leaflets constantly**: Almost always humidity. The plant is shedding its fronds as a stress response.
- **Pale, washed-out colour**: Too much direct [[Sunlight]] or needs [[Fertiliser]]

## Repotting and Division

Repot annually in spring when roots start to emerge from the drainage holes. [[Boston Fern|Boston Ferns]] can also be divided — pull the root ball apart into two or more sections and pot separately. This is a good way to multiply your collection or pass one on to someone with a suitably humid bathroom.

## Outdoors

In mild climates, [[Boston Fern|Boston Ferns]] can be moved outside in a shaded spot in summer. The natural air humidity and gentle rain will do them a world of good. Bring them in well before the first frost.
