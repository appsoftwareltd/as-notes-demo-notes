# Orchid

*Phalaenopsis* spp. — the Moth Orchid. The bestselling houseplant in the world, perpetually gifted, perpetually killed, perpetually misunderstood. The [[Orchid]] has an undeserved reputation for being difficult. It isn't difficult. It simply requires you to stop doing things to it every five minutes.

## The Big Secret

The number one way people kill an [[Orchid]] is by overwatering it. Orchid roots are adapted to drying out between rains in their native tropical habitats. They live in bark and air, not soggy soil. If you treat yours like a regular houseplant and keep it constantly moist, the roots will rot and it will die looking very sorry for itself.

Less is more. This is the entire philosophy of [[Orchid]] care.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Bright indirect [[Sunlight]] — an east or west-facing windowsill is ideal; no harsh direct sun |
| [[Watering]] | Every 7–10 days; soak thoroughly, then let it drain completely and dry out |
| [[Soil]] | Orchid bark mix — never regular potting [[Soil]] |
| Humidity | Prefers 50–70% humidity; appreciates a pebble tray with water underneath |
| Feeding | Orchid-specific [[Fertiliser]] weekly at quarter-strength when in growth |

## After the Flowers Drop

This is the moment most people panic. The blooms fall off and the plant sits there looking like a sad stick. This is normal. It is not dead. It is resting.

Cut the flower spike down to just above the second or third node from the base — you may get a new spike from that node. Or cut it to the base and let the plant put energy into root and leaf growth before blooming again in 6–12 months.

Keep giving it the right conditions and it *will* rebloom. Patience is the skill this plant teaches.

## Common Problems

- **Yellow leaves**: Usually [[Overwatering]] or root rot. Remove from pot, inspect roots, cut off any black/mushy ones with sterilised scissors, and repot in fresh bark.
- **Wrinkled, limp leaves**: Underwatering or severely dehydrated roots. Soak the roots in lukewarm water for 30 minutes.
- **No reblooming**: Not enough light, or roots are unhealthy. Move to a brighter spot and inspect the roots.
- **Aerial roots escaping the pot**: Completely normal. Do not stuff them back in. They are supposed to do that.

## Repotting

Repot every 1–2 years, or when you can see the bark has broken down into fine particles. Use proper orchid bark, not potting mix. Transparent pots are popular because they let you check root health without disturbing the plant.

## Varieties

The *Phalaenopsis* genus is the most common, but specialist orchid growers deal in hundreds of genera including *Cattleya*, *Dendrobium*, *Oncidium*, and the spectacular *Vanda*. These have more specific requirements and are best treated as a hobby in themselves.
