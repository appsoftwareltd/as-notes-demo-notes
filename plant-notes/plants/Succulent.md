# Succulent

[[Succulent|Succulents]] are [[Plants]] that store water in their fleshy leaves, stems, or roots. They evolved in arid, inhospitable environments, and have consequently developed the ability to survive extended periods without [[Watering]] — which makes them the perfect [[Plant]] for people who travel, forget, or simply have very good intentions but terrible follow-through.

Do not mistake "low maintenance" for "no maintenance." [[Succulent|Succulents]] have very clear opinions about water and light, and they will die quietly but definitively if you ignore them.

## The Cardinal Sin: Overwatering

If there is one thing to understand about [[Succulent|Succulents]], it is this: **they would rather be dry than wet.** [[Overwatering]] is by far the leading cause of [[Succulent]] death. A [[Succulent]] sitting in wet [[Soil]] will develop [[Root Rot]] with enthusiasm, and by the time you notice, it's usually too late.

**Water only when the [[Soil]] is completely dry.** In summer, this might be every 2–3 weeks. In winter, every 4–6 weeks. Possibly less. Possibly just don't water it at all from November to February and it'll be absolutely fine.

## Light Requirements

[[Succulent|Succulents]] need **a lot** of [[Sunlight]]. A south-facing windowsill is ideal. Insufficient light causes them to "etiolate" — stretch toward the light, getting pale and elongated and frankly a bit embarrassing compared to their natural compact form.

## Soil and Drainage

[[Succulent|Succulents]] must be in fast-draining [[Soil]]. Use a dedicated succulent/cactus mix, or add generous perlite to regular potting compost. Drainage holes in the pot are **non-negotiable**. A pot with no drainage hole and a [[Succulent]] is a slow-motion tragedy.

## Feeding

A small amount of [[Fertiliser]] in spring is appreciated. Use a low-nitrogen, specialist succulent feed. Do not feed in autumn or winter. The [[Plant]] is resting. Leave it alone.

## Popular Varieties

- **Echeveria** — rosette-shaped, comes in extraordinary colours, very photogenic
- **Haworthia** — tolerates lower light better than most, ideal for less sunny spots
- **Aloe vera** — also usefully medicinal; keep it in [[Sunlight]] and don't [[Overwatering|overwater]] it
- **Jade Plant** — tree-like, very long-lived, extremely satisfying
