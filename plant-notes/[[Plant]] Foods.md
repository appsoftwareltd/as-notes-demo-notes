# Plant Foods

[[Plants]] eat light, water, and nutrients from [[Soil]]. They do not eat toast, pizza, or the leftover pasta you thought about giving them. Please stop pouring coffee on your plants. They hate it.

## The Big Three Nutrients

Every bag of [[Fertiliser]] you ever buy will show three numbers — the NPK ratio. Here's what that means:

- **N — Nitrogen**: Promotes leafy, lush green growth. Great for foliage plants like [[Monstera]] and [[Spider Plant]]. Too much and you get all leaves, no flowers.
- **P — Phosphorus**: Supports root development and flowering. Important for getting your [[Peace Lily]] to actually produce flowers.
- **K — Potassium**: Overall plant health and disease resistance. Think of it as the plant's multivitamin.

## When to Feed

[[Plants]] are hungry during their growing season (spring and summer) and considerably less so during autumn and winter, when they're essentially in hibernation mode and just want to be left alone. The [[Season Guide]] has more detail on this.

General rules:
- Feed every 2–4 weeks during spring/summer
- Don't feed at all in autumn/winter (you'll just be wasting [[Fertiliser]] and confusing the plant)
- Never feed a stressed or newly repotted plant — it's like offering someone a five-course meal immediately after surgery

## Types of Plant Food

### Liquid Fertiliser
Mixed into water and applied during [[Watering]]. Fast-acting, satisfying to use. See [[Fertiliser]] for recommendations.

### Slow-Release Granules
Mixed into the [[Soil]] at [[Repotting]] time. Feeds for several months. Excellent for people who will absolutely forget to do liquid feeds.

### Organic Options
Worm castings, compost, seaweed extract. They smell interesting. The [[Plants]] love them.
