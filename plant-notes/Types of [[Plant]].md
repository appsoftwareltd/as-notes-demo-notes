# Types of Plant

There are, conservatively, too many types of [[Plant]]. But don't panic. You only need to know about the ones you're likely to actually encounter in a garden centre, supermarket, or your colleague's suspiciously well-decorated desk.

## By Difficulty Level

### Easy Mode (Basically Indestructible)

These [[Plants]] have accepted humanity and evolved to survive our worst habits:

- **[[Succulent]]** — stores water in its leaves and judges you silently for three months before dying if you overwater it
- **[[Spider Plant]]** — thrives on neglect, produces offspring chaotically, has never met a window it didn't like
- **[[Peace Lily]]** — droops dramatically when thirsty but bounces back the moment you water it, like a tiny theatrical friend
- **[[Dandy Lion]]** — you've seen how many dandy lions there are around right?
- **[[ZZ Plant]]** — practically immortal, tolerates low light and infrequent watering
- **[[Snake Plant]]** — also known as "Mother-in-Law's Tongue," it survives almost anything
- **[[Aloe Vera]]** — survives neglect, soothes burns, and makes you feel like a herbalist even though you bought it at Tesco
- **[[Cast Iron Plant]]** — named for a reason. You'd have to actively try to kill this one
- **[[Jade Plant]]** — supposedly brings good luck and money. Results on the money front remain unverified

### Normal Mode (Requires Basic Effort)

- **[[Monstera]]** — stunning, rewarding, needs [[Watering]] occasionally and access to a reasonably bright window
- **[[Pothos]]** — grows quickly, tolerates a range of light conditions, and forgives the occasional missed watering
- **[[Philodendron]]** — low-maintenance and adaptable, perfect for beginners
- **[[Rubber Plant]]** — bold, glossy leaves that make any room look like a magazine cover. Likes bright indirect light
- **[[Chinese Evergreen]]** — tolerant, colourful, and won't hold a grudge if you forget about it for a bit
- **[[Calathea]]** — gorgeous patterned leaves that fold up at night like it's tucking itself into bed
- **[[Dracaena]]** — tall, elegant, and surprisingly forgiving. The quiet achiever of the plant world
- **[[Parlour Palm]]** — has been making living rooms look fancy since the Victorian era and shows no signs of stopping

### Hard Mode (Why Are You Doing This To Yourself)

- Orchids — not covered here. Go and find a specialist. Take water.
- Bonsai — a commitment measured in decades. Respect.
- **[[Fiddle Leaf Fig]]** — beautiful but temperamental, needs consistent care and the perfect spot
- **[[Maidenhair Fern]]** — ethereally pretty, dies if you so much as look at it wrong. Or breathe near it. Or exist
- **[[String of Pearls]]** — adorable trailing succulent that will melt into mush the moment your watering schedule slips
- **[[Croton]]** — stunning colours, but it will drop every single leaf if you move it two inches to the left

### You're Not Qualified (Seriously, Walk Away)

These [[Plants]] are not for hobbyists. They are for people who have dedicated their lives to horticulture, own a greenhouse, and possibly talk to their plants in Latin.

- **[[Ghost Orchid]]** — doesn't even grow in soil. Needs a specific host tree, exact humidity, and the blessing of a swamp. You will never see one flower indoors
- **[[Corpse Flower]]** — blooms once every 7–10 years, smells like death when it does, and needs a conservatory the size of a small church
- **[[Lithops]]** — look like pebbles. Water them even slightly wrong and they split open or rot. Deceptively evil
- **[[Sensitive Plant]]** — folds its leaves when you touch it, which is charming until you realise it's doing that because it's stressed and you are the problem
- **[[Venus Flytrap]]** — yes, it eats flies. No, your house is not a bog. It needs distilled water, full sun, and a dormancy period. You are not ready
- **[[Pitcher Plant]]** — another carnivore. Requires near-constant humidity and will punish you emotionally if conditions aren't perfect
- **[[Black Bat Flower]]** — looks incredible, needs tropical greenhouse conditions, and will refuse to bloom just to spite you
- **[[Welwitschia]]** — technically only two leaves. Can live for over a thousand years in the Namib Desert. Cannot live on your windowsill in Croydon

## By How You Feel About Watering

- Hate watering → [[Succulent]], [[Snake Plant]], [[ZZ Plant]], [[Cast Iron Plant]], [[Jade Plant]]
- Fine with weekly watering → [[Monstera]], [[Peace Lily]], [[Pothos]], [[Rubber Plant]], [[Dracaena]]
- Enjoy watering as a hobby → any of them, you'll be fine
- Want a real challenge → [[Maidenhair Fern]], [[Calathea]], [[String of Pearls]] — and good luck to you
