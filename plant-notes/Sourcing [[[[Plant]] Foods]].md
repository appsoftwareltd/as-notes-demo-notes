# Sourcing Plant Foods

So you've decided to feed your [[Plants]]. Excellent. Now the question is: where does one acquire these [[[[Plant]] Foods]]? Multiple options exist, ranging from "extremely convenient" to "slightly unhinged but effective."

## Garden Centres

The classic option. Walk in, become immediately overwhelmed by the selection, buy three things you didn't need, and leave. Garden centres stock:

- A bewildering range of [[Fertiliser]] products
- [[Soil]] and composts
- Slow-release granules
- Staff who actually know things (use this resource)

**Verdict**: Great. Go. Leave your credit card in the car.

## Supermarkets

Most large supermarkets carry a small range of houseplant food, usually in small bottles near the pots and [[Watering Can]] accessories. It works perfectly well. Nobody needs to know.

**Verdict**: Fine for basic [[[[Plant]] Foods]]. Convenient. Slightly uninspiring.

## Online

The internet will sell you everything. You can get enormous, professional-grade quantities of fertiliser for a fraction of the garden centre price. Useful if you have many [[Plants]] or have fully committed to the bit.

Recommended search terms:
- "NPK liquid fertiliser"
- "houseplant feed concentrate"
- "worm castings" (if you want to go organic and slightly weird about it)

**Verdict**: Excellent value. You will end up with more [[[[Plant]] Foods]] than you can use in your lifetime.

## Making Your Own

Compost bins. Worm farms. These are real things that real people do. If you're maintaining a worm farm specifically to feed your [[Plants]], you've achieved a level of commitment that deserves respect.

See [[Soil]] for more on compost and organic growing media.

**Verdict**: Deeply committed. Environmentally excellent. Absolutely valid.

## Steal From a Friend

If someone you know has a large bag of slow-release granules, a small handful is usually welcomed. This is practically a tradition.

**Verdict**: 10/10, sustainable, builds community.
