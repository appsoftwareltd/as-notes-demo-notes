# Plant

A plant is a living organism that cannot walk away from its problems. This is the main difference between a plant and you. Plants deal with adversity by either thriving heroically or going yellow and crispy — there is no middle ground.

## What Makes Something a Plant?

Scientifically speaking, plants are multicellular organisms that convert sunlight into food via photosynthesis. Less scientifically speaking, they're the things you keep buying and then feeling guilty about.

Key plant characteristics:
- Needs [[Watering]] (but not *too much* [[Watering]], see: [[Overwatering]])
- Needs [[Sunlight]] (but not too much direct sun either, honestly plants are exhausting)
- Grown in [[Soil]] which they will outgrow and need [[Repotting]]
- Occasionally attacked by [[Pests]]
- Occasionally shows [[Yellow Leaves]] as a cry for help

## Why Have Plants?

Research confirms that plants make you happier, improve air quality, and make your flat look considerably more put-together than it actually is. They are also excellent listeners and never criticise your life choices.

## Types of Plant

There are approximately 390,000 known species of plant. You will probably end up with a [[Monstera]], a [[Succulent]], a [[Peace Lily]], and a [[Spider Plant]], because those are the ones they sell at every garden centre and supermarket.

For a structured overview, see [[Types of [[Plant]]]].

## Feeding Your Plant

[[Plants]] need nutrients to survive, just like you do, except their diet consists of [[[[Plant]] Foods]] rather than toast and anxiety. See [[[[Plant]] Foods]] for details, and [[Sourcing [[[[Plant]] Foods]]]] for where to get the stuff.

## Seasonal Behaviour

[[Plants]] respond to the seasons and may slow down, drop leaves, or look frankly alarming during winter. This is usually fine. Consult the [[Season Guide]] to avoid making rash decisions like "I think it's dead, I'll bin it" in January.
