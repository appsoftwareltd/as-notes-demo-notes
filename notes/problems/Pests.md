# Pests

At some point, something small, uninvited, and extremely difficult to evict will move into your [[Plants]]. This is not a reflection of your cleanliness or competence as a person. [[Pests]] are simply a part of the [[Plant]] ownership experience, and the faster you identify them, the faster you can begin your campaign of eradication.

## The Usual Suspects

### Spider Mites
Tiny, often barely visible, produce fine webbing on the undersides of leaves. Thrive in hot, dry conditions. Hate humidity. **Treatment**: mist the leaves regularly, wipe with a damp cloth, use insecticidal soap or neem oil. They are infuriating but beatable.

### Fungus Gnats
Small flies hovering around your [[Soil]]. The adults are just annoying. The larvae live in the [[Soil]] and eat roots, which is less annoying and more genuinely bad. **Treatment**: allow [[Soil]] to dry out more between [[Watering]] (fungus gnat larvae need moist [[Soil]] to survive). Sticky yellow traps catch the adults. Neem oil drenches kill the larvae.

### Scale Insects
Brown, armoured blobs that attach to stems and look disconcertingly like part of the plant. They suck sap. **Treatment**: scrape them off with a fingernail or cotton bud dipped in rubbing alcohol. Persistent infestations need systemic insecticide.

### Mealybugs
White, fluffy-looking, appear in leaf joints and on stems. Look like tiny dots of cotton wool. Actively unpleasant. **Treatment**: dab individually with rubbing alcohol on a cotton bud, or use neem oil spray. Repeat weekly.

### Aphids
Small green (or black, or white) insects usually found on new growth, which they find irresistible. **Treatment**: spray off with water, use insecticidal soap. Easier to deal with than most.

## General Principles

- **Inspect new [[Plants]]** before bringing them home. [[Pests]] in a shop are [[Pests]] in your home.
- **Quarantine new plants** for two weeks away from your existing collection. Even if they look fine.
- **Act immediately** when you spot [[Pests]]. They reproduce quickly and spread to neighbouring [[Plants]].
- **Good plant health helps**. Stressed [[Plants]] — from [[Overwatering]], bad [[Sunlight]], or poor [[Soil]] — are more vulnerable.

## Neem Oil: The All-Purpose Solution

Neem oil is an organic, naturally-derived pesticide that disrupts pest life cycles. It deals with spider mites, mealybugs, aphids, and more. It smells strongly of garlic and wet dog, which is the price you pay for not using chemicals. Your [[Plants]] will be fine with it. Your kitchen might need a window open.
