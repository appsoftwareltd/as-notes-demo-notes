# Yellow Leaves

Your [[Plant]] has [[Yellow Leaves]]. You are alarmed. You should be — at least a little. [[Yellow Leaves]] are a [[Plant]]'s way of communicating that something is wrong, like a passive-aggressive text message from someone who won't just say what the problem is.

The frustrating truth is that [[Yellow Leaves]] can mean many different things. Let's narrow it down.

## Is This Normal?

First: check where the yellow leaves are. If they are at the **bottom of the plant, on the oldest growth**, and appear only occasionally — this is normal. [[Plants]] shed old leaves. It is not a crisis. Remove the yellow leaf and move on with your day.

If yellowing is happening on **new or mid-plant growth**, something is actually wrong.

## Common Causes

### [[Overwatering]]
The most frequent culprit. Yellowing usually starts on lower leaves and progresses upward. The [[Soil]] will feel soggy or smell musty. Stop [[Watering]] immediately. Check for [[Root Rot]].

### Underwatering
Leaves yellow and then become crispy, starting from the tips. The [[Soil]] will be completely dry and pulling away from the sides of the pot. Solution: water thoroughly.

### Too Little [[Sunlight]]
Pale, washed-out yellow — the plant looks faded rather than sick. Move it somewhere brighter. See [[Sunlight]].

### Nutrient Deficiency
If the yellowing follows the leaf veins (green veins, yellow between them), this is often a sign the [[Plant]] needs feeding. Give it [[Fertiliser]].

### Natural Leaf Ageing
See above. Old leaves go yellow. That's just how [[Plants]] work. Don't panic.

### [[Pests]]
Check the undersides of leaves for anything that shouldn't be there — webbing, white fluff, small insects. Deal with the [[Pests]] and leaves should recover.

## What to Do

1. Check the [[Soil]] — wet or dry?
2. Check the light — enough [[Sunlight]]?
3. Check the leaves — any [[Pests]]?
4. Check when you last fed it — is it due for [[Fertiliser]]?
5. Remove yellow leaves cleanly, they won't recover
6. Solve the underlying issue

Yellow leaves are a symptom, not a disease. Find the actual problem and fix that.
