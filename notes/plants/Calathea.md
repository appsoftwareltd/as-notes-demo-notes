# Calathea

*Calathea* spp. — the [[Calathea]]. No plant on this list is more ornamental, more particular, or more quietly passive-aggressive than the [[Calathea]]. Its leaves are extraordinary: boldly patterned in dark and light greens, sometimes purpled underneath, sometimes striped, sometimes spotted — nature behaving as if it had access to a design agency.

In return for this spectacular appearance, the [[Calathea]] has a list of demands. That list is not short.

## The Folding Trick

[[Calathea|Calatheas]] are nyctinastic — they fold their leaves upward at night and open them again in the morning. This is controlled by a small joint at the base of each leaf stalk adjusting in response to light. It is genuinely delightful to witness. It is also the reason some people call them "prayer plants," though strictly speaking that name belongs to the related *Maranta* — the [[Calathea]] is far too sophisticated to share a nickname.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Medium to bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; no direct sun; no deep shade |
| [[Watering]] | Every 1–2 weeks; keep [[Soil]] lightly moist but not soggy |
| [[Soil]] | Well-draining but moisture-retaining potting mix |
| Humidity | High — this is non-negotiable. The [[Calathea]] is from the rainforest |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] monthly in spring/summer; skip winter |

## The Humidity Problem

This is the part where many well-meaning [[Calathea]] owners encounter difficulty. [[Calathea|Calatheas]] are native to tropical rainforests and genuinely need high humidity. In a dry centrally-heated flat, they will develop **brown, crispy leaf edges** — the botanical equivalent of a pointed complaint.

Solutions, in roughly ascending order of commitment:

1. **Misting** — helps temporarily; not a complete fix but better than nothing
2. **Pebble tray with water** — place the pot on a tray of wet pebbles; evaporation raises local humidity
3. **Plant grouping** — [[Plants]] collectively raise the humidity around them
4. **Humidifier** — the nuclear option, and the one that actually works properly

## Watering

[[Calathea|Calatheas]] are fussy about water chemistry as well as quantity. Tap water containing fluoride or chlorine can cause leaf tip browning. Use filtered water, rainwater, or leave tap water overnight to let the chlorine dissipate. Keep the [[Soil]] consistently lightly moist — not soggy, never bone dry.

[[Overwatering]] causes [[Root Rot]]; underwatering causes wilting and crispy edges. The window between the two is narrower than you'd like.

## Common Problems

- **Brown, crispy leaf edges**: Low humidity — the single most common [[Calathea]] complaint. Address humidity first
- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: [[Overwatering]], poor drainage, or occasionally natural ageing of older leaves
- **Curling leaves**: Underwatering or very low humidity; water it and assess
- **Faded leaf patterns**: Too much direct [[Sunlight]] — the patterns wash out in harsh light
- **Brown spots**: Possible sunburn, inconsistent [[Watering]], or cold draughts

## Repotting

[[Repotting|Repot]] in spring when rootbound, into a pot only one size larger. The [[Calathea]] does not want to be overwhelmed by excess [[Soil]] — too much growing medium holds moisture and increases the risk of [[Root Rot]].

## Popular Varieties

- **Calathea orbifolia** — large round leaves with silver-green stripes, the most dramatic and the least tolerant of neglect
- **Calathea lancifolia (Rattlesnake Plant)** — long wavy leaves with dark green spots on lighter green; purple underside
- **Calathea medallion** — round, boldly patterned, purple underneath; a popular choice for good reason
- **Calathea ornata (Pin Stripe)** — dark green leaves with fine pink-white lines, very elegant
- **Calathea zebrina** — bold zebra stripes; needs particularly high humidity; not for beginners
