# Peace Lily

*Spathiphyllum* — the [[Peace Lily]]. If [[Plants]] had a "most likely to succeed in terrible conditions" award, the [[Peace Lily]] would win it consistently. It is the ultimate low-light, low-maintenance, high-drama houseplant. It droops when thirsty, recovers immediately when watered, and somehow still looks magnificent. 

Fundamentally, the [[Peace Lily]] is a plant that performs feelings. You will find this either charming or exhausting.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Low to medium [[Sunlight]] — genuinely fine in dim rooms |
| [[Watering]] | Every 1–2 weeks; allow top inch of [[Soil]] to dry |
| [[Soil]] | Regular potting mix |
| Humidity | Appreciates misting; native to tropical rainforests |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] monthly in growing season |

## The Dramatic Droop

When a [[Peace Lily]] needs water, it does not hint subtly. It droops. Visibly. Theatrically. Leaves hang down like it's auditioning for a role in a sad film. This is alarming but not dangerous — water it, and within an hour it will have perked back up completely, as if nothing happened.

This is useful if you are the sort of person who needs a visible reminder. Think of it as a living [[Watering]] alarm.

Do not let it droop repeatedly — chronic stress weakens the plant and eventually it becomes less forgiving.

## The White Flowers

Those white "flowers" are technically spathes — modified leaves surrounding a yellow-white spike. They appear in spring and sometimes autumn. To encourage flowering:

- Ensure it's getting enough (if dim) [[Sunlight]]
- Keep it slightly root-bound (don't [[Repotting|repot]] it unless necessary)
- Feed it with [[Fertiliser]] during spring

## Toxicity Warning

[[Peace Lily]] is toxic to cats and dogs. Keep it somewhere your pets can't reach it, or choose a different [[Plant]] altogether. No plant is worth a vet bill and attendant guilt.

## Common Problems

- **Brown leaf tips**: Too dry, too much direct [[Sunlight]], or fluoride in the water — try leaving tap water overnight before [[Watering]]
- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: [[Overwatering]] or natural ageing of older leaves at the base
- **No flowers**: Needs more [[Sunlight]], or more [[Fertiliser]], or is happy but unexcited
