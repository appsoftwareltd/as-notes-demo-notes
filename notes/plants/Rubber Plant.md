# Rubber Plant

*Ficus elastica* — the [[Rubber Plant]]. Tall, glossy, architectural, and deeply aware of the effect it has on a room. The [[Rubber Plant]] is the plant equivalent of someone who walks into a party and immediately becomes the most interesting person there, without appearing to try.

It was, at one point, actually used to produce rubber. Now it produces extremely photogenic Instagram content. Arguably a more valuable contribution.

## What It Looks Like

Large, oval, deeply glossy leaves in dark green — or burgundy, or variegated cream-and-green depending on the variety. The new growth emerges from a red-pink sheath, which unfurls dramatically and then dries and falls off. This is normal. It is not dying. It is simply being theatrical about growing.

New leaves have an almost waxy sheen that dulls slightly as they mature. This is also normal.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; avoid dark corners and harsh direct sun |
| [[Watering]] | Every 1–2 weeks in summer; less in winter; allow top inch to dry |
| [[Soil]] | Well-draining potting mix |
| Humidity | Appreciates occasional misting; tolerates normal indoor humidity |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] every 2–4 weeks in spring/summer |

## Light

[[Rubber Plant|Rubber Plants]] want bright, indirect [[Sunlight]]. A spot near a window with filtered light is ideal. They will tolerate slightly lower light but will grow more slowly and produce smaller, less impressive leaves — which defeats rather the point.

Dark green varieties handle lower light better than variegated ones, which need more light to maintain their markings.

## Watering

Water when the top inch of [[Soil]] is dry. The [[Rubber Plant]] does not want to sit in wet [[Soil]] — [[Overwatering]] leads to [[Root Rot]] and [[Yellow Leaves]]. In winter, reduce [[Watering]] considerably. If the leaves start to droop slightly, water it. If the leaves are yellowing, you're probably already overdoing it.

## The Milky Sap

[[Rubber Plant|Rubber Plants]] produce a white latex sap that oozes from any cut or damaged leaf. This sap is a skin irritant and toxic to pets. Wear gloves when pruning, keep the plant away from curious animals, and don't let children chew on it. Wipe leaves with a damp cloth occasionally — they collect dust and dusty leaves photosynthesize less efficiently, which is an embarrassment for everyone.

## Common Problems

- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: Most commonly [[Overwatering]] — check [[Soil]] and reduce [[Watering]] frequency
- **Leaf drop**: Cold draughts, sudden temperature changes, or being moved. The [[Rubber Plant]] hates being moved. Pick a spot and commit
- **Leggy, sparse growth**: Not enough [[Sunlight]] — find a brighter window
- **Brown edges**: Low humidity or inconsistent [[Watering]]
- **No growth**: Normal in winter; in summer, check [[Sunlight]] and [[Fertiliser]]

## Pruning and Shaping

[[Rubber Plant|Rubber Plants]] can be pruned to control height and encourage branching. Cut just above a leaf node. Wear gloves due to the sap. The plant will branch from below the cut, gradually developing from a single tall stem into something that fills a room with genuine authority.

## Varieties

- **'Robusta'** — classic dark green, the most common
- **'Burgundy'** — very dark, almost black-red leaves, extremely dramatic
- **'Tineke'** — green with cream-white variegation and pink edges
- **'Ruby'** — dark burgundy with pink-red new growth, the most theatrical option available
