# Dracaena

*Dracaena* spp. — the [[Dracaena]]. Tall, elegant, sculptural, and reassuringly easygoing. While other [[Plants]] are busy demanding humidity, filtered water, and constant emotional reassurance, the [[Dracaena]] is quietly getting on with it in the corner, looking incredible, and waiting for you to remember to water it.

The [[Dracaena]] is one of the most popular houseplants in the world, which says something complimentary about humanity's taste.

## What It Looks Like

The most common varieties are tall, slender, and architectural — a woody cane topped with a tuft of long, narrow leaves. The [[Dracaena marginata]] (Dragon Tree) has dark green leaves edged in red or purple. The [[Dracaena]] *fragrans* (Corn Plant) has broader, paler green leaves with yellow stripes. Both look like they belong in a designer office. Both are much more tolerant than their appearance suggests.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Medium to bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; tolerates lower light |
| [[Watering]] | Every 1–2 weeks; allow top half of [[Soil]] to dry between waterings |
| [[Soil]] | Well-draining potting mix |
| Humidity | Tolerates normal indoor humidity; appreciates occasional misting |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] every 2–4 weeks in spring/summer; none in winter |

## Light

The [[Dracaena]] is adaptable. It prefers medium to bright indirect [[Sunlight]] but tolerates lower light better than many [[Plants]] of similar stature. In low light it grows more slowly and the leaf colouring may become less vivid — variegated varieties fade notably without adequate [[Sunlight]].

Direct sunlight, however, will scorch the leaf tips and edges. Stick to indirect.

## Watering

Water when the top half of the [[Soil]] has dried out. The [[Dracaena]] prefers not to sit in wet [[Soil]], and [[Overwatering]] is the most reliable way to cause problems. In winter, reduce [[Watering]] substantially — the plant is growing very slowly and needs much less.

Like the [[Calathea]] and [[Peace Lily]], [[Dracaena|Dracaenas]] are sensitive to fluoride in tap water, which causes brown leaf tips. Use filtered water or leave tap water overnight to off-gas the chlorine. This makes a noticeable difference.

## Natural Leaf Drop

Lower leaves on [[Dracaena|Dracaenas]] naturally yellow and drop as the plant grows, revealing the bare cane below. This is completely normal, not a sign of distress, and is actually how the plant achieves that distinctive tall-cane-with-topknot silhouette over time. Do not panic. Do not repot. Just remove the yellow leaves when they arrive and carry on.

## Common Problems

- **Brown leaf tips**: Very common — caused by fluoride in tap water, low humidity, or inconsistent [[Watering]]. Switch to filtered water and trim the tips
- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: Lower leaves yellowing is natural aging; if it's higher up the plant, suspect [[Overwatering]]
- **Soft, mushy cane**: [[Root Rot]] — serious. Remove from [[Soil]], cut away rotten roots and cane sections, dry out, and repot in fresh dry mix
- **Pale, washed-out leaves**: Too much direct [[Sunlight]] or, in variegated types, insufficient light — an impressive way to be wrong in two opposite directions
- **Pests**: [[Dracaena|Dracaenas]] are occasionally bothered by spider mites and scale insects, particularly in dry conditions — check regularly and treat with appropriate methods. See [[Pests]] for options

## Repotting

[[Repotting|Repot]] every 2–3 years in spring, going up one pot size. [[Dracaena|Dracaenas]] do not need frequent [[Repotting]] and generally prefer being slightly pot-bound.

## Varieties

- **Dracaena marginata (Dragon Tree)** — the classic tall, spiky one with red-edged leaves. Very popular and tough
- **Dracaena fragrans 'Massangeana' (Corn Plant)** — broader, arching leaves with a yellow-green centre stripe
- **Dracaena reflexa 'Song of India'** — yellow-edged leaves, more bushy than upright
- **Dracaena sanderiana (Lucky Bamboo)** — not actually bamboo, technically a [[Dracaena]], frequently found growing in vases of water
