# Spider Plant

*Chlorophytum comosum* — the [[Spider Plant]]. If you have ever begged your neighbour for a cutting, or had someone press a small plantlet into your hands at a party "because it's really easy," there is a very high chance it was a [[Spider Plant]].

The [[Spider Plant]] is the plant kingdom's most enthusiastic reproducer. Give it a year and it will be producing cascading runners with baby plants — called **spiderettes** or **plantlets** — at such a rate that you begin to wonder if this was all a mistake.

It isn't. They're wonderful.

## Why Spider Plants Are Great

- Nearly unkillable
- Actively thrives in indirect light and cool rooms
- Grows in almost any [[Soil]]
- Produces free new [[Plants]] constantly
- Said to improve air quality (evidence: contested; vibes: positive)
- Asks for very little in return

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Bright to medium indirect [[Sunlight]]; no direct sun |
| [[Watering]] | Every 1–2 weeks; tolerates occasional drought |
| [[Soil]] | Any standard potting [[Soil]] |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] every 2–4 weeks in spring/summer |
| [[Repotting]] | When roots fill the pot; typically yearly in a vigorous plant |

## The Babies

The spiderettes that dangle from long runners are the [[Spider Plant]]'s great gift to the world. To propagate:

1. Wait until the plantlet has visible small roots
2. Pop it into a small pot of moist [[Soil]]
3. Keep moist until established
4. Give it away, because you will have too many within months

## Common Problems

- **Brown tips**: The most common symptom. Causes include fluoride in water, low humidity, or inconsistent [[Watering]]. Trim the tips if cosmetically annoying.
- **No spiderettes**: Usually needs to be slightly pot-bound and getting sufficient [[Sunlight]]
- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: [[Overwatering]], or the older leaves at the base naturally yellowing — both normal

## Varieties

The classic is green with a white stripe. There are also fully green varieties, and curly versions (*Chlorophytum comosum 'Bonnie'*) which are compact and slightly more interesting. All are equally easy.
