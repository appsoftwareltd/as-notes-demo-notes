# Chinese Evergreen

*Aglaonema* spp. — the Chinese Evergreen. One of the most forgiving, adaptable, and colourful plants you can keep indoors. The [[Chinese Evergreen]] is the kind of plant that rewards neglect with a shrug and keeps looking fantastic anyway. Available in shades ranging from deep forest green to vivid pink, red, and silver — there is an Aglaonema for every aesthetic.

## Why It's Perfect for Beginners

The [[Chinese Evergreen]] tolerates more mistakes than almost any other houseplant:

- Low light? Fine.
- Forgot to water for two weeks? Probably fine.
- Inconsistent temperature? Mostly fine.
- Too much love and attention? Actually this is where it might struggle — see overwatering.

The only thing it genuinely does not tolerate is cold draughts and temperatures below 15°C. Keep it away from cold windowsills in winter and it will almost certainly outlive you.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Adaptable: dark green varieties tolerate low light; variegated/pink varieties need brighter indirect light to maintain colour |
| [[Watering]] | Allow top 2–3 inches of [[Soil]] to dry out before watering; every 7–14 days depending on season |
| [[Soil]] | Well-draining potting mix; perlite addition helps prevent waterlogging |
| Humidity | Tolerates normal room humidity; appreciates occasional misting |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] every 4–6 weeks in spring/summer; little to none in winter |

## The Colour Rule

As a general guide: **the more colourful and variegated the variety, the more light it needs.** A deep green *Aglaonema commutatum* will soldier on in a dark corner. A hot-pink or red *'Siam Aurora'* needs bright indirect light to maintain its colouring, otherwise it will gradually revert toward green.

Match variety to location accordingly.

## Common Problems

- **Yellow leaves**: Almost always [[Overwatering]]. Ease off and allow the [[Soil]] to dry more before the next watering.
- **Brown leaf tips**: Low humidity or fluoride in tap water. Try using filtered or rainwater.
- **Pale/faded colour**: Insufficient light, especially in variegated varieties. Move to a brighter spot.
- **Drooping stems**: Either very thirsty or very cold. Check the temperature of its location first.

## Toxicity

[[Chinese Evergreen]] is toxic to cats, dogs, and humans if ingested — it contains calcium oxalate crystals. Keep it out of reach of [[Plants|plant]]-curious pets and small children.

## Varieties

The *Aglaonema* genus includes dozens of cultivars in an extraordinary range of colours. Popular choices include:

- *'Silver Bay'* — silvery-green, very tolerant of low light
- *'Siam Aurora'* — deep green with vivid red edges
- *'Pink Dalmatian'* — green with pink speckles
- *'Emerald Beauty'* — classic deep green with silver markings
