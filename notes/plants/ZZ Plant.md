# ZZ Plant

*Zamioculcas zamiifolia* — the [[ZZ Plant]]. If you have ever worried that you are constitutionally incapable of keeping a plant alive, the [[ZZ Plant]] is here to prove you wrong. It tolerates low light, infrequent [[Watering]], neglect, bad vibes, and months of being completely ignored. It was almost certainly engineered to make the rest of the plant world feel inadequate.

The name "ZZ" comes from its scientific name, which most people give up pronouncing approximately two syllables in.

## Why the ZZ Plant Is Basically Immortal

The secret to the [[ZZ Plant]]'s supernatural resilience lies underground: it grows from large potato-like rhizomes that store water and nutrients. These allow the plant to survive extended droughts with the sort of calm indifference that would be admirable in a human.

This means you can go on holiday, forget it exists for a month, come back, water it once, and have it looking perfectly fine. This is not an exaggeration.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Low to bright indirect [[Sunlight]] — genuinely fine in dim rooms |
| [[Watering]] | Every 2–4 weeks; allow [[Soil]] to dry out completely |
| [[Soil]] | Well-draining mix; add perlite if in doubt |
| Humidity | Not bothered. Seriously. Leave it alone |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] 2–3 times in spring/summer; none in winter |

## Light (Or Lack Of It)

The [[ZZ Plant]] tolerates remarkably low light — it will survive in a dim corner where other [[Plants]] would quietly give up. It prefers bright indirect [[Sunlight]], and with better light it will grow faster and more lushly, but it will not sulk in the dark.

What it cannot tolerate is prolonged direct sun, which will scorch the leaves and make the whole thing look like a cautionary tale.

## Watering

Water thoroughly, then let it dry out completely before watering again. The rhizomes will carry it through the gaps. [[Overwatering]] is the one genuine threat to a [[ZZ Plant]] — too much water causes [[Root Rot]], at which point the plant's legendary resilience becomes somewhat less impressive.

If in doubt, do not water it. It will be fine.

## Common Problems

- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: Almost always [[Overwatering]]. Check the [[Soil]], reduce watering frequency, resist the urge to "help"
- **Leggy growth**: Not enough [[Sunlight]] — move it somewhere brighter
- **Very slow growth**: Normal in low light or during winter. It's not dying; it's resting and judging you
- **No new growth for months**: Also normal. The [[ZZ Plant]] is not in a hurry

## A Word of Warning

The [[ZZ Plant]] is toxic if ingested — to pets and humans alike. Keep it somewhere your cat, dog, or toddler can't get to it. This is the one area where the plant stops being entirely cooperative.

## Propagation

You can propagate from leaf cuttings placed in [[Soil]] or water, though this is a famously slow process. A single leaf cutting can take many months to develop a rhizome. If you are patient, it is deeply rewarding. If you are not patient, just buy another one — they're inexpensive and everywhere.
