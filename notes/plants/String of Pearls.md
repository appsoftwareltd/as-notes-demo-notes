# String of Pearls

*Curio rowleyanus* (formerly *Senecio rowleyanus*) — [[String of Pearls]]. The name is entirely accurate. The [[Plant]] produces long, trailing stems from which small, perfectly spherical leaves hang like a curtain of green beads. It is one of the most visually distinctive [[Plants]] available, and it is not difficult to see why it became popular: the effect is simultaneously peculiar and genuinely beautiful.

Each "pearl" is a modified leaf, swollen with stored water — the spherical shape maximises volume while minimising surface area, reducing water loss. This is clever engineering, and it means the [[Plant]] is well-suited to dry conditions. It is also, unfortunately, easily undone by [[Overwatering]].

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; 3–4 hours of direct morning sun is beneficial |
| [[Watering]] | Every 2–3 weeks in summer; every 4–6 weeks in winter — allow [[Soil]] to dry completely |
| [[Soil]] | Cactus/succulent mix with added grit or perlite; drainage is critical |
| Humidity | Tolerates dry air; do not mist the pearls |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] once in spring, once in midsummer |

## The Watering Trap

[[String of Pearls]] is one of the plants most commonly killed by [[Overwatering]]. The pearls are plump when the [[Plant]] is healthy and well-fed with water — watering before they show any sign of wrinkling is almost always too soon. Wait until the pearls begin to look very slightly deflated or shrivelled, then water thoroughly, allow to drain completely, and put the plant back.

Sitting in damp [[Soil]] causes [[Root Rot]] with frustrating speed. The stems will go mushy and detach. There is rarely a recovery once it has progressed.

## Displaying

[[String of Pearls]] looks best in a hanging basket or elevated on a shelf where the trails can drape freely. The cascading strands can reach considerable lengths — 60–90cm is achievable — and look spectacular in a bright, airy room.

Do not place it where the pearls will be touched frequently; they are fragile and will detach from jostling. The sap is mildly toxic, so keep out of reach of pets and small children.

## Flowering

In good conditions — bright light and a cooler winter rest — [[String of Pearls]] will produce small, white, dome-shaped flowers with a faint cinnamon scent. They are small and not particularly dramatic, but they are a reliable sign that the plant is content.

## Common Problems

- **Mushy or translucent pearls**: [[Overwatering]]; allow to dry completely and assess root health
- **Shrivelled, deflated pearls**: Thirsty — water thoroughly
- **Dropping pearls**: Usually draughts, temperature changes, or being moved; also a sign of distress from [[Overwatering]] or drought
- **Long bare stems with pearls only at the tips**: Not enough [[Sunlight]]; move to a brighter position

## Propagation

[[String of Pearls]] propagates very easily. Lay strands across the surface of slightly damp cactus [[Soil]], or insert 4–5 cm sections of stem directly into the [[Soil]]. Roots develop quickly. Pin the strands down with a hair clip or bent wire if they won't stay put. Within a few weeks, new pearls will begin to emerge. This is deeply rewarding.

A related species, *String of Bananas* (*Curio radicans*), has banana-shaped rather than spherical leaves and is often considered more vigorous and easier to keep with the same care requirements.
