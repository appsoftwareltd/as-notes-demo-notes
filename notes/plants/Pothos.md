# Pothos

*Epipremnum aureum* — [[Pothos]], or as it is also known, Devil's Ivy. The "Devil's" part refers to the fact that it is incredibly difficult to kill — it keeps growing even in the dark, even without water, even when all observable evidence suggests it should be dead. If there is a plant that has genuinely accepted its fate as a companion to busy, forgetful humans, it is this one.

It cascades. It climbs. It produces cheerful heart-shaped leaves with aplomb. It asks for almost nothing back. This is a good deal.

## Why "Devil's Ivy"?

The name comes from its tendency to stay green even in the dark — which, spiritually, feels like something a devil would do. In practice, it just means your trailing shelf plant will look perfectly fine in conditions that would finish off a lesser [[Plant]].

It is also remarkably difficult to kill through neglect alone. You'd have to actively commit to destroying it.

## Care Summary

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Light | Low to bright indirect [[Sunlight]]; variegated types need more light |
| [[Watering]] | Every 1–2 weeks; allow top inch of [[Soil]] to dry |
| [[Soil]] | Standard potting mix; well-draining |
| Humidity | Tolerates normal room humidity; misting appreciated but not required |
| Feeding | [[Fertiliser]] monthly in spring/summer |

## Light and Variegation

Plain green [[Pothos]] manages low light better than most [[Plants]]. Variegated varieties — white, yellow, or marble-patterned — need more light to maintain their colouring. In low light, the variegated leaves will gradually revert to green, which is the plant essentially giving up on aesthetics and prioritising survival. You can't blame it.

## Trailing, Climbing, and Training

[[Pothos]] is naturally a climbing plant in the wild. Indoors, it happily trails from shelves, hangs from baskets, or climbs a moss pole if you provide one. Climbing produces larger leaves. Trailing looks magnificent. Both are valid.

Trim as needed to keep it tidy and bushy — cuttings propagate so easily it becomes a moral obligation to share them.

## Common Problems

- **[[Yellow Leaves]]**: Usually [[Overwatering]], occasionally nutrient deficiency — check [[Soil]] moisture first
- **Brown, mushy stems**: [[Root Rot]] from [[Overwatering]]; cut back affected sections, let [[Soil]] dry out
- **Pale or bleached leaves**: Too much direct [[Sunlight]] — move it somewhere with indirect light
- **Leggy with small leaves**: Not enough [[Sunlight]]; the plant is stretching toward whatever light it can find
- **Loss of variegation**: Needs more light to sustain the patterns

## Propagation

[[Pothos]] is one of the easiest [[Plants]] to propagate in existence. Take a cutting below a node (the small brown nub on the stem), place in water, and watch roots appear within a week or two. Then pot it up. Then give the spare to someone you know. Then do it again.

Within a single year you can go from one [[Pothos]] to an embarrassing number, which is frankly the intended outcome.

## Popular Varieties

- **Golden Pothos** — the classic; green with yellow marbling
- **Marble Queen** — white and green, needs brighter light
- **Neon Pothos** — lime green, almost aggressively cheerful
- **Manjula** — creamy white variegation, rounder leaves
- **N'Joy** — crisp white and green patches, neatly defined
