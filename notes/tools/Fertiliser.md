# Fertiliser

[[Fertiliser]] is supplementary plant nutrition — the extra nutrients your [[Plants]] need that the [[Soil]] alone can't reliably provide, especially after a year in the same pot. If [[Watering]] is feeding your [[Plants]] water, [[Fertiliser]] is giving them their vitamins. Except some vitamins are actually mandatory.

See [[[[Plant]] Foods]] for the full science of what's in [[Fertiliser]] and why plants need it.

## When to Use

Feed only during the growing season — **spring and summer**. Do not feed in autumn and winter. The [[Plant]] is dormant, it cannot use the nutrients, and you'll just be building up salt residues in the [[Soil]] for no benefit.

**Never feed a stressed, dry, or newly repotted [[Plant]]**. When a [[Plant]] is already struggling, [[Fertiliser]] is more insult than help.

## Types of Fertiliser

### Liquid Concentrate
Mixed into water and applied when [[Watering]]. Fast-acting. Easy to adjust dosage. The most common form for houseplants.

*How to use*: Add the recommended amount to your [[Watering Can]] and water as normal. Do not exceed the dosage. "More is more" is not a rule that applies here — over-fertilising causes leaf burn and can damage roots.

### Slow-Release Granules
Small pellets mixed into [[Soil]] at [[Repotting]] time, or pushed into the top of the [[Soil]]. Release nutrients gradually over 3–6 months. Ideal for people who will absolutely forget to do liquid feeds.

### Foliar Spray
Diluted fertiliser sprayed directly onto leaves. Useful for quick uptake of micronutrients. Not a replacement for root feeding.

## NPK: The Three Numbers

Every [[Fertiliser]] label shows three numbers: the NPK ratio.

- **N** (Nitrogen): Leaf and stem growth — higher N for foliage [[Plants]] like [[Monstera]], [[Spider Plant]]
- **P** (Phosphorus): Root development and flowering — higher P for [[Peace Lily]] flowers
- **K** (Potassium): General plant health, disease resistance

A balanced fertiliser (e.g. 20-20-20) suits most houseplants. Specialist formulas exist for specific [[Plants]] but aren't strictly necessary for a home collection.

## Signs You Need to Feed

- Slow or no growth during spring/summer
- Pale, washed-out leaves (see also [[Sunlight]])
- [[Yellow Leaves]] with green veins — classic nitrogen or iron deficiency
