# Watering Can

A [[Watering Can]] is a vessel for carrying and delivering water to your [[Plants]] with some degree of control. You could, in principle, use a cup. Or a jug. Or cup your hands. But a [[Watering Can]] — particularly one with a long, narrow spout — makes [[Watering]] significantly more pleasant and less soggy.

## Does It Actually Matter?

Yes, more than you'd think. The right [[Watering Can]] will:

- Let you water at the [[Soil]] level without splashing leaves (wet foliage can invite fungal problems)
- Give you control over flow rate
- Reach plants at the back of shelves without requiring a yoga pose
- Make you feel like a proper plant person

## Types of Watering Can

### Long Spout (Indoor)
The classic houseplant [[Watering Can]]. A long, narrow spout lets you direct water precisely to the [[Soil]], navigate around other [[Plants]], and water hanging baskets without drenching everything beneath them. This is what you want.

**Capacity**: 1–2 litres is plenty for indoor use. You don't need to carry a bucket.

### Wide Spout (Outdoor)
For garden beds and outdoor [[Plants]]. Less suitable indoors — less control, more splash.

### Self-Watering Inserts
Not technically a [[Watering Can]], but: self-watering pots with reservoirs, or wicking systems, can reduce [[Watering]] frequency significantly. Good if you travel a lot. Your [[Succulent]] will still judge you for it.

## Copper vs Plastic vs Metal

- **Plastic**: Lightweight, cheap, perfectly functional. No shame.
- **Metal/Galvanised**: Heavier, looks beautiful on a shelf, slightly impractical. Worth it for aesthetics if you're that way inclined.
- **Copper**: Extremely pretty. Extremely expensive. Copper has some mild antimicrobial properties but your [[Plants]] cannot tell the difference.

## Capacity Recommendations

| Collection Size | Can Capacity |
|---|---|
| 1–5 plants | 0.5–1 litre |
| 5–15 plants | 1–2 litres |
| 15+ plants | 2–5 litres, or consider a nearby tap and bucket system |

## Care

Rinse your [[Watering Can]] occasionally, especially if you add [[Fertiliser]] to it — residues can build up and block the spout. Leave the lid off to keep it dry and prevent mould inside.
