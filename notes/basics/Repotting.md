# Repotting

[[Repotting]] is the process of moving your [[Plant]] into a larger container with fresh [[Soil]]. Your [[Plant]] will need this every one to two years as it grows, because roots need space and the old [[Soil]] eventually becomes tired and nutrient-depleted — relatable content.

## When to Repot

Signs it's time:

- **Roots coming out of drainage holes** — the plant is literally trying to escape
- **Roots circling the inside of the pot** — visible when you slide the plant out
- **The plant wobbles or falls over** — top-heavy with not enough soil anchoring it
- **Water runs straight through** — soil so root-bound there's nowhere for water to sit
- **It hasn't been repotted in two years** — just do it

When NOT to repot:
- Winter — [[Plants]] are dormant and won't recover as easily
- When the plant is already stressed, sick, or fighting [[Pests]]
- Immediately after buying a new plant (give it two weeks to settle)

See the [[Season Guide]] — spring is the ideal time.

## How to Repot

1. Water the [[Plant]] 24 hours before — damp roots slide out more easily
2. Choose a new pot **one size up** (2–5cm wider in diameter). Going too big drowns roots in soggy [[Soil]]
3. Add fresh [[Soil]] to the bottom of the new pot
4. Gently remove the [[Plant]] from its old pot — tip, squeeze, wiggle
5. Inspect the roots. Trim any black or mushy ones (signs of [[Root Rot]])
6. Place in new pot, fill around it with fresh [[Soil]], press lightly
7. [[Watering|Water thoroughly]] and place somewhere not in direct [[Sunlight]] for a few days while it recovers
8. Do NOT feed it with [[Fertiliser]] for at least a month — newly repotted plants don't need the extra pressure

## Pot Material Matters

- **Terracotta**: breathes well, dries out faster. Good for [[Succulent]] and anything prone to [[Root Rot]]
- **Plastic**: retains moisture longer. Better for moisture-loving [[Plants]] like [[Monstera]]
- **Self-watering pots**: sophisticated. Expensive. Works well for the perpetually forgetful
