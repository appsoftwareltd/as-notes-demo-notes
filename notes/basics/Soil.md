# Soil

[[Soil]] is where your [[Plants]] live. It is not, as some have attempted, gravel. It is not sand. It is not garden soil taken directly from outside, which is too heavy, probably has things living in it, and will compact into a solid brick in a pot. Please use proper potting mix.

## What Makes Good Potting Mix?

Good houseplant [[Soil]] balances three things:

1. **Nutrients** — food for the [[Plant]] to draw on (see [[[[Plant]] Foods]] for supplementary feeding)
2. **Drainage** — excess water needs to escape so roots don't sit wet and get [[Root Rot]]
3. **Aeration** — roots need oxygen as well as water; compacted soil suffocates them

## Types of Potting Mix

### General Purpose Houseplant Compost
Does what it says. Fine for [[Monstera]], [[Spider Plant]], [[Peace Lily]], and most common houseplants.

### Succulent & Cactus Mix
Very free-draining, gritty. Mimics desert conditions. Essential for [[Succulent]] varieties, which will rot in regular [[Soil]] if you [[Roll the Dice|water too much]].

### Orchid Bark
Chunky, airy, nothing like conventional [[Soil]]. Don't use it for [[Plants]] that aren't orchids. (Orchids don't count as normal. They never did.)

### Homemade Mix
You can blend general compost with perlite (little white volcanic balls) to improve drainage. This makes you look like you know what you're doing, and also works.

## Changing the Soil

[[Soil]] breaks down over time and loses structure and nutrients. [[Repotting]] is the time to refresh it completely. See [[Repotting]] for when and how to do this.

## The Bit About Garden Soil

You will be tempted to use garden soil. It is free and close-by and there is a lot of it. Don't. It's too dense, it may contain weed seeds and pathogens, and it will compact horribly indoors. Pay the two pounds fifty for a bag of compost.
